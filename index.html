Hello!

I'm Jack Corp, a researcher of early medieval religious and social history. Welcome to my portfolio.

Research Interests

I focus on the history of early medieval belief, from magic and demons to the cult of saints, in rural socities. Other interests include: historiography and the philosophy of history; empire and political imagination; and the construction of cosmologies.

I'm also interested in comparing ideas of sanctity and the holy between the fluid, contested, and imperial religious environments of Carolingian Francia and Tang-Song China.

Undergraduate Religious Studies Association Spring Symposium

2022

At Indianna University Bloomington, I presented my honors research about how the supernatural manifested in the social environments of the Carolingian countryside by investigating how rural communities engaged with saints and demons. 

“The Contest Countryside: Peasant Engagement with the Supernatural in Carolingian Francia” 

Phi Alpha Theta Centennial Conference

2021

At this digital conference sponsored by the University of Arkansas, I argued for a new reading of the Jacquerie of 1358, which I developed primarly through the untranslated royal remissions of Charles V. Through a dissection of near-contemporaneous sources and deconstruction of poor historiographical models emerges a paradigm of revolt which illuminates the Jacques as independent actors, not without leadership, organized around the communal ties of rural life to challenge the structures of aristocratic authority.  

"Portraits of Revolt: Reapproaching the Jacquerie of 1358"

Phi Alpha Theta Biennial Conference

2020

In San Antonio, Texas, I presented my research about magic in the Middle Ages. I argued that medieval society drew upon a cumulative pool of definitions and boundaries to form a spectrum from which magic provided a shared approach to answer questions of identity and create a sense of order in a dynamic world.

"Conjured Identities Within Conjured Lines: Magic as a Tool for Understanding in the Middle Ages"

Midwest Political Science Undergraduate Research Conference

2019

In Omaha, Nebraska, at Creighton University, I presented a paper on the ethno-nationalist populism pervading French and Hungarian politics. As a first-year student, the conference was a meaningful introduction to the world of academia.

"A People Left Behind: Ethno-Nationalist Populism in France and Hungary" 

EXPERIENCES

Research Assistantship, May - August 2021

Under the guidance of Dr. Michael Verney, I received a $4,000 Drury Fusion Learning Grant to transcribe letters from the North Pacific Exploring Expedition (1853-1856) housed in the National Archives in Washington, D.C. From naval and political to post-colonial, the Expedition represents a dynamic interplay of numerous histories Dr. Verney aims to record in his upcoming book project.

Archival Internship, Janurary - August 2020

At the History Museum on the Square in Springfield, MO, I appraised, preserved, and sorted donated materials, while developing archival database skills. I also conceptualized and implemented an exhibit on on the history of Springfield's segregated park system.

Undergraduate Teaching, August - December 2020

I developed and led an honors reading seminar about the cultural conceptions of magic. My primary responsibility was the facilitation of group discussion.
